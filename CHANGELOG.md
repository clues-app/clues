# Clues - Version Log

Short notes on what's new. The full manual is the [User Guide](USER_GUIDE.md).

## 2026-07-11

- **Scrub through time.** Time-series charts get a *time scrubber*: a slider under the chart that reveals the lines only up to the chosen date - drag it while presenting and history animates forward under your control. View-only, works with the forecast overlay, and the AI drives it with `chart_display` → `"time_scrubber": true`.
- **The bar race.** *Bar race* in + Add a chart (Over Time) - or switch any time chart's type: at each date, horizontal bars show every category's value, re-ranking live as time plays - press ▶ Play or drag the timeline. Values carry forward when a category has no data at a date, colors follow your theme, and the whole animation is saved with the session and plays in Present mode - the deck moment PowerPoint can't export. The AI can build one: a race needs a date, a measure, and a category.
- **The race shows its history - bump-chart style.** Beside the racing bars, a *trail* panel draws each category's trajectory into a fixed full-time window, with a dotted "now" marker sweeping right as the race plays. Default view is **Rank** over time - crossing lines are overtakes - with each line end labeled with its current standing ("#2 France ▲1"); switch to **Value** for raw trajectories ("France · 12.2"), or Off. AI: `chart_display` → `"race_trail"`.
- **Forecasting on time series.** A new forecast toggle extends each line into the future: a dotted Holt-Winters projection with an honestly widening 95% band, seasonality detected from your data's cadence (daily → weekly cycle, monthly → yearly...), horizon automatic or yours to set. Everything is computed deterministically from the data in the file - reproducible by anyone you share with, no black box, no invented numbers - and the chart carries a caption saying exactly what it is. The hover names the model and the points it was fit on. AI: `chart_display` → `"forecast": true` or a number of periods.
- **Fix: "match" notes within one spec.** An AI spec that creates a presentation note on one chart and then styles the others with `"match"` was wrongly rejected - the validator judged against the pre-import session instead of the spec's own sequence. Ops apply in order, so a note created earlier in the same spec is now a legal match source (and the error message explains the ordering when it isn't).
- **Ranked bars.** Bar charts get a Sort control - high → low (the ranking view), low → high, or the data's own order. Sorting is by the bar metric itself, orientation-aware (horizontal bars read top-down), never confused by an overlaid combo line, and AI-driveable (`chart_bars` → `"sort": "desc"`). The one chart.guide rule Clues couldn't follow, now it can.
- **Chart-craft principles, built in.** Suggested charts now name the data relationship they serve (Correlation, Distribution, Ranking...) as a small tag, and every auto-built chart's note carries a matching craft principle - "the shape is the story," "bars start at zero," "correlation is not causation." The AI context teaches the same rules (form follows the relationship, integrity constraints, takeaway titles and one action color for storytelling decks). Distilled from the FT Visual Vocabulary, chart.guide, and Ann K. Emery's storytelling checklist.
- **A new default look, and one-click themes.** Fresh charts now open in a muted editorial palette - desaturated but separable, kind to projectors and printers. And Appearance gains a **Looks** gallery: *Editorial* (the default, one click back after experiments), *Scientific* (colorblind-safe Okabe-Ito, survives grayscale print), and *Corporate Slate* (deep slate, soft periwinkle, warm amber, Roboto typography). Each look is a normal styling spec under the hood - it validates, records in AI responses, and undoes there - and sessions you already styled keep their exact look.
- **Your description briefs the AI.** The dataset description you write on the Description tab now travels in the copied AI context, marked as the user's own words about the data and goals - in Analyze mode as primary context, in Restyle mode for tone and naming.
- **AI provenance on every import.** Specs are now asked to carry `produced_by` (which model / Gem made them) and optional `notes` (2-3 sentences on the approach); both are stored with the session and shown in AI responses - so someone opening the file next year knows what was done, by what, and why.
- **Timeline slider on time-series charts.** A new toggle in the Over Time toolbar adds a draggable mini-timeline under the chart - grab the handles to zoom into any period, presentation-friendly. It changes that chart's view only (the dataset and other charts are untouched); the AI drives it with `chart_display` → `"range_slider": true`.
- **House styles round-trip completely.** *Copy current styling* now also captures region-map settings (boundary set, location-code system, custom GeoJSON), histogram bin width, the dragged position of the style-field legend, and the sunburst/treemap theme-colors switch - and the matching ops apply them back. A captured look reproduces fully on another dataset.
- **Fix:** log axes now apply inside faceted (small-multiple) panels too.


## 2026-07-10

- **Log axes.** A small *log* checkbox next to the axis min/max controls turns an axis logarithmic (base 10): scatter and density X and Y (GDP per capita vs life expectancy, at last), bar value axis, time-series Y, and ECDF X. Manual ranges still take plain data values, the AI drives it with `chart_axis` → `"log": true`, and the context reports which axes are log. No more re-uploading log-transformed data.
- **In-chart notes drag reliably now.** A note added to a chart you were already looking at couldn't be dragged until the chart rebuilt (a Plotly quirk: reacting to changes skips the editing switches). Fixed - notes are movable the moment they appear. The Annotate Chart menu also reaches scatter matrices, heatmaps, grids, and ECDF charts now, so AI-placed notes there can be edited or removed.
- **The AI can place charts on a grid.** The Restyle context now teaches placement geometry: the presentation band's current width in px, how to convert grid columns ("note in columns 2-4, chart in 5-11") into `chart_box` width/left and note width, and that this geometry renders in Presentation view. And when an imported spec sets deck sizes or notes while you're in the Workspace view, a toast now tells you where to look - it used to read as "the spec did nothing."
- **Suggested charts open in one batch.** Each suggestion on the Overview now has a checkbox (all ticked by default); one **Open checked** button creates them together - suggestions matching a chart you already have are skipped, never duplicated - and lands you on the first chart group.
- **Almost-JSON is accepted.** AI replies with the classic near-misses - a trailing comma, `//` comments, smart quotes from the chat window - now import cleanly instead of failing with "could not parse JSON at position 5849." When a spec truly can't be read, the error quotes the offending line and names the usual suspects, so pasting it back to the AI gets a real fix. The copied context also states the strict-JSON rules up front.
- **Present from anywhere.** The Present button now shows on every tab - including Raw data and Overview. Clicked from a data tab, it jumps to the deck's first section and goes straight on stage; exiting restores the tab you were on.
- **Deep professional restyle.** A checkbox in *Copy AI context → Restyle* turns the request into a one-pass, presentation-grade makeover brief: palette via theme slots, one typography hierarchy, decluttering, field-name cleanup (`body_mass_g` → "Body Mass (g)"), structural fixes like the bar+line axis gap, and short how-to-read notes on dense charts - with orders to state assumptions instead of asking questions. If your assistant holds your standing house style (a Gem or custom GPT), the brief tells it to apply that first and only fill the gaps. Pair it with the contact sheet: context + image out, one comprehensive spec back.
- **No more surprise save folders.** Remembered save folders are now reused silently only after you've confirmed them once per session: the first save of each kind opens the folder picker *at* the remembered folder (keep it with one click, or steer elsewhere); later saves that session stay one-click. Files no longer vanish into a folder you picked weeks ago in another project - browsers only ever reveal the folder's short name, so the fix is confirming, not reporting.
- **Give your AI eyes: the contact sheet.** One click in *Copy AI context → Restyle* saves a single PNG tiling every presentation chart, each tile labeled with the chart's ref. Charts you never scrolled to are rendered in the background automatically before the shot. Paste it into the chat next to the context and the assistant can finally *see* the deck - judge crowding, clashes, and consistency - and answer with changes addressed to exact charts. Made for "make my whole deck look professional" requests with minimal back-and-forth.
- **Time-axis ranges that speak date.** Over-Time charts finally apply a manual X range - a new X control takes a year (2008) or a date (2008-06-01), the reset button clears it, and the AI's `chart_axis` understands dates and `y2`. Numeric bounds on a date axis are read as years, killing the classic "my time axis starts in 1970" trap (Plotly reads bare numbers as milliseconds since 1970). The AI context now shows each manual range's actual values, so "restore my axis" has a baseline to restore to.
- **Breathing room for the bar+line combo's second axis.** A new gap control (the ↔ box next to the line's Aa size box, or `chart_bars` → `line_axis_gap`) pushes the line axis's caption away from its numbers, and the chart margin grows automatically so nothing clips.

## 2026-07-09

- **Settings that were silently ignored now work.** Region-map transparency honors the map opacity setting (point maps already did); the per-series opacity knob now reaches parallel-coordinates ribbons, 3D-stacked facet layers, split-violin dots, and 3D histogram silhouettes; series line-width reaches measure-colored map outlines and 3D silhouettes; jitter strength applies in 3D like in 2D; KPI, gauge, and grid-heatmap numbers finally respect your saved field formats (decimals, prefix, suffix); and the global tick-angle setting reaches heatmap labels.
- **The AI knows what you're looking at.** The copied context now lists your active filters (and says plainly that charts show the filtered subset), each series' full current style - line width, dash, area fill, and your alias, so "make Gentoo's line thicker" has a baseline - and every in-chart annotation with its id, so "move my threshold line" edits it instead of adding a second one.
- **Your AI now sees every chart - in both modes.** *Analyze & coach* context now includes the "Charts already in this session" list (it used to ship only with *Restyle*), with a picker for which charts to include - so the assistant builds on your charts instead of recreating them. And the per-chart descriptions now carry everything that was invisible: heatmap/parallel/matrix measures, sankey and category-flow stages, grid rows/columns, KPI aggregation, map color-by-measure and blink fields, histogram bin size, time-series band and axis side, marginals, error-bar type, manual axis ranges, and series you hid via the legend (so the AI stops asserting a hidden series is displayed).
- **Your AI now sees the whole bar chart.** The copied AI context describes a bar chart's line overlay (the bar+line combo's second measure), its split series, and its orientation - so "make the right axis caption bigger" lands on the right chart instead of "this chart doesn't have Body Mass." And that caption is now sizable: a small **Aa** box next to the Line picker (or `chart_bars` → `line_axis_size`) sets the line axis's text size per chart.
- **Draw inside your charts.** New *Annotate Chart* menu on scatter, bar, histogram, box, violin, and time-series charts: **shaded areas** (a target range behind the data, a season between two dates), **marker lines** (a vertical "policy change" line, a horizontal threshold), and **notes pinned to a data point** with an arrow. Positions take numbers, dates, or category names; everything saves with the session and prints in exports. The AI gets three matching ops (`chart_shade`, `chart_marker`, `chart_note`), each annotation addressable by id. And they're **draggable like the legend** - move a note's text, re-aim its arrow, slide a caption; the new spot is saved.
- **Reorder the deck's sections.** Drag a section heading (Relationships, Over Time...) onto another in the all-charts view and the presentation plays in your order - menu and arrows follow.
- **Hide the analysis tables.** One checkbox in Chart & Axis Layout turns the per-family analysis tables off entirely, for a charts-only deck.
- **Fix:** inline title edits no longer accumulate invisible bold/italic tags around the text.
- **Deleted rows are kept, not erased.** A source-update deletion now marks the row **Deleted** in Raw data (excluded from charts, restorable with a comment) instead of removing it - full history, and per-row revert keeps working.
- **Fresh data inside the presentation.** Decks with a connected source get a refresh button in the presentation bar (amber when updates are pending) - check, review, and accept without leaving Present. Phone viewers finally have a door to current data.
- **Bar charts grow up.** Horizontal or vertical, split by a second category as side-by-side / stacked / overlapping series (true Excel-style series overlap), bar width control, solid / one-color-gradient / colorscale fills, hatch patterns (print-safe in black-and-white), editable borders, error bars that say what they mean (±1 SD, ± SEM, 95% CI - the hover spells out value and *n*), and a second measure overlaid as a line on its own axis - the classic bar+line combo. All AI-controllable (`chart_bars`).
- **Data history scrubber.** A History button on the Raw data tab opens a timeline of the edit journal: pick a measure, drag through history, watch its mean and the row count move - the entry that moved them is shown at every step. "When did this number change?" is now a ten-second question.
- **Compact edit history.** Auto-updating sources write hundreds of log entries a day; the new Compact button folds old history into the starting snapshot - the data stays identical, newer history keeps replaying, and signatures keep verifying (the cut can never fold entries a signature protects).
- **Keyboard slides.** In Present mode, Left/Right arrows (and PageUp/PageDown - presenter remotes) step chart by chart.
- **Ready-made examples - try one on your phone.** The [examples/](examples/) folder ships live demo sessions: the [Palmer Penguins presentation](https://clues.ai/examples/penguins.clue) and a [self-updating earthquake dashboard](https://clues.ai/examples/earthquakes.clue). On a mobile: download a `.clue`, open [clues.ai](https://clues.ai), tap **Open a shared session**, pick the file - it plays as a full-screen presentation (the earthquake one refreshes itself from the USGS catalog).
- **Blinking map points.** Point maps get a "Blink" field picker: rows where the chosen column is true (true/1/yes) pulse gently on the map - made for live dashboards where the newest events should catch the eye. Respects reduced-motion settings; pauses in hidden tabs.
- **Auto-accepted sources.** A curated source can now apply its updates automatically (optionally checking on a timer) - every change still fully logged like an accepted review, and conflicts with your hand edits still ask first. Made for self-updating sheets that accumulate and correct rather than replace.
- **Live feeds.** Link a source as a *live feed* (instruments, monitoring, rolling windows like the USGS quake CSV): each refresh replaces the data wholesale - optionally on a timer (5/15/60 min) - turning a shared deck into a self-updating dashboard. Curated sources keep the full review-and-accept audit flow; feeds and signatures are mutually exclusive by design.
- **Automatic reconciliation.** A status pill on the Raw data tab continuously verifies that the data equals the original file plus exactly the documented edit log - undocumented changes, inconsistent log entries, and removed log entries are named row by row, no passphrase needed. And when a source update touches a cell you also edited by hand, the review dialog now lists it in a Conflicts group so the overwrite is a conscious choice.
- **Audit trail grows up: custody signature chains.** Sign the data *as it is now*, at any stage - your signature certifies that every later change is documented. Colleagues add their own signatures on top; signatures interlock so nobody can quietly alter or strip an earlier one, and only the newest can be removed, by its owner, with its passphrase. Pick any signature and run its integrity check to verify "my snapshot plus only documented edits since."
