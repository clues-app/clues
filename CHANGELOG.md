# Clues - Version Log

Short notes on what's new. The full manual is the [User Guide](USER_GUIDE.md).

## 2026-07-16

- **The region-map ticker names the region.** When a replay step reveals exactly one row (metric-ordered replays reveal one region at a time), the ticker reads REGION - value; chunked steps keep the value alone.
- **Region maps replay too, and replays loop a set number of times.** Choropleths gain the same replay: regions recolor step by step with the cumulative per-region average (color scale pinned so the bar never rescales mid-loop) - order by a date, or by the metric itself to light regions up lowest-to-highest. Both map replays now run a chosen number of loops (x1/x2 default/x3/x5/infinite) and then rest at the full picture. AI: chart_display replay_loops.
- **Map replay: watch the dots arrive.** Point maps gain a replay control: pick a field (usually the date) and the dots appear one by one in that order, ACCUMULATING into the full picture with a ticker showing the current value, looping until paused. Speed choices, per-chart, saved with the session - a shared deck can open animating. AI: chart_display replay_field / replay / replay_speed.
- **No more title/note riding over the plot on phones.** The compact height clamp squeezed presenting cards around their already-scaled plots, pushing the title row and caption note into the chart. On stage, cards now wrap their content (the deck scaling already fits plots to the screen); the clamp still protects the non-presenting compact view.
- **Landscape phones get a real deck.** Compact screens already flattened notes below their charts, but width caps and left offsets still applied - a landscape phone showed a squeezed chart beside dead space, under an oversized touch modebar. Cards now fill the row on compact screens and the modebar stays out of the way on stage.
- **Showcase: the 3D scatter sits out of the presentation.** WebGL scenes can freeze rendering on phones; the chart stays in the workspace (Esc) and can be re-included from its chart menu.
- **Looks now really apply.** A preset Look used to change only the default palette, which explicit per-category colors always beat - in any styled session the Apply button looked dead. Applying a Look now clears the per-category color overrides first (new theme_repaint op, also available to your AI), so the palette visibly repaints; symbols, opacities, and unlinked charts keep their styling, and the whole thing undoes in Dataset -> AI responses.
- **AI specs may say "750px".** A px suffix on any numeric op parameter is now normalized away before validation - the most common AI formatting habit no longer costs a rejection round trip.
- **Notes are read-only on stage.** In Present mode presentation notes show no toolbar and take no keystrokes until you double-click one to edit; clicking away re-locks it. Entering Present always starts locked. (The title slide is unchanged.)
- **Opening a deck on another monitor migrates it instead of breaking it.** The authoring view used to re-stamp the design screen without converting the stored pixel layout - after which Present stopped scaling and big-monitor decks clipped on smaller screens (and the corruption saved back). Now the whole composition rescales to the new screen at that moment: what you see is what you keep, on any monitor.
- **Decks fit sideways on smaller monitors.** Sessions saved with a design height but no design width (before the horizontal-scaling build - the embedded showcase included) never scaled horizontally, so pixel-placed charts, notes, and images clipped on narrower screens. The missing width is now inferred from the composition itself (its rightmost pixel edge, with an aspect floor) at load and stamped at save - verified zero overflow at 1366x768, 1440x900, and 1920x1080.
- **Hiding the panel always opens the deck.** Presentation view now engages whenever the left panel is hidden on a chart tab - even with a 2-column workspace. The deck itself lays out 1-column as always, and bringing the panel back returns your workspace grid exactly as you organized it.
- **Your deck, from the first click.** Fresh sessions now open with the house presentation look: the blue gradient page (#081a44 to #203f88), Roboto Condensed section headings, and every newly added or suggested chart right-placed (about 49% wide at 43% from the left) with its presentation note preset to Roboto 24 white. All of it stays editable in Appearance and per chart - these are just the starting values. Existing sessions keep their saved look.
- **chart_duplicate op.** Your AI can now clone a chart: an independent copy - fields, styling, canvas, geometry, note - lands right after the original, optionally retitled.
- **Visible slide arrows.** The Present bar chart stepper uses filled triangles in a stronger tone instead of the faint chevrons.
- **New notes default to the left again** (top-default reverted by request).
- **A deleted-then-reshown note shows its placeholder.** Deleting all text in a note left invisible HTML residue behind; an emptied note now stores as truly empty, so Show Presentation Note always brings up a visible type-here box.
- **The empty-note placeholder is centered** so a fresh note is obvious at a glance. (Notes render in Presentation view only, at their chosen position - the workspace stays chart-only.)
- **New presentation notes appear above the chart.** A freshly shown, empty presentation note now defaults to the top position so it cannot hide off the left edge; existing notes keep their saved positions. The show/hide item is offered in every view except Present mode, where it has no effect (a note with content always shows on stage); in the workspace the note itself appears once you switch to Presentation view.
- **clues.ai now opens straight into the showcase presentation.** The embedded session autoloads at boot (returning visitors resume their own autosaved work; Clear Session still offers the empty start screen). On phone-sized screens whatever restores is forced into Present mode - the deck plays full-screen instead of the small-screen gate, and the presentation bar folder button still opens any other .clue. Lean app exports stay plain: no embed, no autoload.
- **White-ink charts no longer get blazing gridlines.** When a chart has an ink color (its own chart_canvas ink or the deck melt) but no explicit grid setting, the gridlines now default to that ink at deck softness instead of inheriting the ink at full strength.
- **No more workspace flash when a shared file opens presenting.** Portable HTML files (and autosave restores) now restore the session before the first paint, so the first visible frame is the presentation - previously the empty workspace shell flashed for a fraction of a second.
- **Analysis tables start collapsed on stage.** In Present mode the tables toggle now shows collapsed header bars; expand the one you want in place. Expansion is remembered per family while presenting and resets to collapsed at the start of every show. The workspace checkbox and its open/closed state are unaffected.
- **Remove unused images in one click.** Appearance -> Images (deck assets) now shows a Remove N unused button whenever the library holds images no chart or title slide points at - the usual leftovers after re-adding images under new filenames.
- **Optimized images stay optimized.** Importing an already-small webp/png/jpeg (within the 1280px cap) used to re-encode it through a canvas and often make it BIGGER; the original bytes are now kept whenever they are smaller. Reminder: re-adding an image replaces the old one only when the FILENAME matches exactly, extension included - a penguin.png next to a new penguin.webp are two library entries; remove the old one in Appearance → Images.
- **Keep a lean app.** A new icon button next to Load Session saves a copy of Clues with the embedded dataset stripped - megabytes lighter, just the app, and still fully able to Share (interactive). The start screen hides the embedded card when there is nothing embedded, and says **Embedded:** rather than Showcase - because a shared interactive file embeds YOUR session there, not the demo.

- **Parallel-chart text is readable on any deck.** Parallel-coordinates axis names, ticks, ranges - and the category colorbar that acts as its legend - are drawn by the trace itself, out of reach of the usual axis styling. All of it now follows the same ink chain as everything else (per-chart canvas ink, deck harmony auto-contrast, theme).
- **Sankey node labels too.** The third and last chart type with trace-drawn text joins the contrast chain preemptively.
- **Categories (parallel categories) labels read on dark decks.** Like the parallel-coordinates chart, parcats draws its dimension and category labels inside the trace - they now follow the contrast chain (per-chart canvas ink, deck harmony, theme).
- **Faceted charts read on dark decks.** A faceted chart's main title and its shared legend are drawn as page text (not by Plotly), so the transparent-deck ink never reached them - dark-on-dark, as reported. Both now follow the same contrast chain as everything else (a chart's own title color still wins).
- **Fix: the stage tables button actually toggles.** The Present-bar analysis-tables button could never win against the Appearance master checkbox (unchecked = permanently hidden), and a table collapsed in the workspace stayed invisible on stage. The two are now independent: the checkbox governs the Workspace view, the stage button alone governs Present - and turning it on opens the tables regardless of their workspace collapse state.
- **Formatting over formatted text finally wins.** Applying a font or size to a selection that already contained earlier formatting could silently lose: the old inner styling outranked the new wrapper (inline beats inherited), so the pick seemed to do nothing - the root of the long "font/size does not work" saga, and notes accumulated invisible layers with every attempt. Applying now clears the same property from everything inside the selection first, like any real editor. Verified on the exact reported note: nine legacy layers, select-all, one pick - every text piece lands on the chosen size.
- **Melt mode: the link and menu buttons are visible when idle.** Their white chips showed light ink on white; neutral buttons now go transparent with ink outlines while active states keep their colored chips.
- **The note toolbar reads like a real text editor now.** The font and size dropdowns display the format OF YOUR SELECTION (or the text at the caret) - select a Courier word and the dropdown says Courier, exactly like Word or Docs. Before, they showed the note's base setting, which read as "stuck on Auto" or "stuck on Roboto" even while selection styling was being applied correctly.
- **Present notes toggle rebuilt.** The stage notes button now shows every chart note that has content (it used to reveal only notes whose per-chart toggle was already on - typically zero, so it looked dead), and toggling notes on a single chart while presenting switches the stage master on instead of fighting it.
- **Note font/size dropdowns never go dead.** After a pick landed on the whole note, choosing the SAME value for newly selected text fired no change event - the control seemed to stop responding. Opening the dropdown now resets it to neutral, so every pick registers (chart notes and the title slide). The dropdown showing "Auto" after styling a selection is by design: it reflects the note's BASE font, which selection styling deliberately leaves alone.
- **The chart menu stays readable in melt mode.** The auto-contrast ink stopped at the ⋮ menu popover, which keeps its solid bright panel - its text keeps the panel's own colors.
- **Toolbar captions follow the deck ink in melt mode.** With charts melted into a dark deck, the chart-controls captions kept their theme color and vanished; they now take the same auto-contrast ink as the charts (dropdowns and inputs keep their own solid backgrounds and stay as they were).
- **Fix (for real this time): selection font & size in notes.** Choosing a font or size for selected note text could still restyle the whole note - the mechanism depended on catching a mouse event before the toolbar stole focus, and real click paths do not guarantee that order. Selection styling no longer depends on event timing at all: Clues continuously remembers the last text selection in each note, and the toolbar styles exactly that - verified end-to-end with real browser clicks on the showcase deck. Applies to chart notes and the title slide alike.
- **Stage toggles in the Present bar.** Four icon buttons while presenting: **analysis tables** (hidden by default on stage), **chart toolbars** (hidden by default - the deck is pure charts until you summon the controls), **working notes** (the amber chart notes, hidden by default; presentation notes always show), and **Melt** (the transparent-charts switch, mirroring Appearance). All four remember their state with the session.
- **Style-legend swatches follow the ink too.** The Style-field legend deliberately draws neutral near-black glyphs so they are not mistaken for data colors - which made them invisible on dark decks. They now use the same contrast chain as their captions.
- **Fix: Clear session clears on the first click.** The delayed autosave could fire in the instant between clearing and reloading, quietly re-saving the session it had just wiped - it took several attempts to win the race. Clearing now silences the pending save.
- **A new showcase.** The embedded Palmer Penguins demo is re-recorded: an image-rich presentation with a title slide - the Presentation door on the start screen now opens into the real thing.
- **Saved sessions always carry their design screen.** Deck scaling needs to know the screen a deck was designed on; sessions saved before the feature (including the current showcase) have no record and scale 1:1. Every save now stamps it (the authoring view already did) - so any .clue saved from today fits itself to whatever monitor presents it.

- **The style legend stays readable on any deck.** The Style-field legend used theme ink regardless of the page behind it - unreadable on a dark presentation background. It now follows the same chain as everything else: a chart_canvas legend/ink color first, then deck harmony auto-contrast, then the theme.
- **The start screen grew up.** The showcase is a card: a title band (Showcase: Palmer Penguins) over two doors - **Workspace** (the everyday workbench) and **Presentation** (straight into the full-screen deck, the best first look). And the upload button now takes .clue files too: Upload CSV, Excel or Clue.
- **The demo opens presenting.** The start screen's showcase button is now a pair: "Load original Palmer Penguins" opens the workbench as before, and a new "▶ Open in Presentation Mode" segment loads the showcase straight into the full-screen deck - the best first look for a new visitor. (The version date is gone from the label; it lives in Help & Guide.)
- **Fix: races honor your Y range.** The race value axis (Trail: Value, the bars panel, and bars-only mode) used to override the toolbar Y from/to with its own computed extent; manual values win now, and unset ends still auto-fit across all frames.
- **Race captions can carry a companion field.** A new Caption + dropdown on race charts appends another column to every line-end caption - its latest value per series at that moment of the race. Pick the date column and each caption shows when that series was last actually observed ("#2 Euro · 1.0842 · 2026-03-04 ▲1"); pick a measure or category and its running value rides along. AI: chart_display race_caption_field.
- **Race data gaps stay gaps, and captions speak your language.** An empty cell used to count as a literal zero in races and bumps, crashing a series to the floor at missing dates - gaps now carry the last real value forward, as intended. And everywhere a race or bump names a series - the line-end captions, the bars, the hovers, the bump rails - your series ALIAS shows instead of the raw column value.
- **The race is now the trail.** Bar-race charts default to the full-width trajectory view - every line end captioned with rank AND value ("#2 France · 12.2 ▲1") - with the racing-bars panel as an opt-in **Bars** checkbox. Playback is much smoother: with bars hidden nothing re-lays-out per frame (the axes are fixed up front, the now-line is data, more frames fit), and a **Speed** control sets the whole race duration - Slow, Normal, Fast, Fastest. AI: chart_display race_bars / race_speed.
- **Slides fit any screen now.** The deck remembers the height of the screen it was designed on; Present mode scales every vertical measurement - chart heights, freely placed note and image positions, the title slide layout - by the ratio to the current screen, both ways: a deck tuned on a tall monitor shrinks to fit a shorter one, and grows into a bigger one, so the next slide no longer peeks into view (and charts that ran a tad too big pull themselves in). Widths scale the same way: percent geometry follows the band as before, and DRAGGED (pixel) widths and offsets now scale by the design-width ratio too. Adjustments made while presenting are stored back in design units, so your original layout never corrupts.
- **Melt charts into the deck - one checkbox.** Appearance gains a switch that makes every chart in Presentation view transparent with faint gridlines and text, axes, legends, and titles auto-colored to CONTRAST with your page background (gradients use the blend). No per-chart work: set your deck color, tick the box, done. Charts individually styled with chart_canvas keep their own settings; AI: layout_style deck_transparent. Facet panels (small multiples) get the same treatment - canvas, ink, and gridlines.

- **Per-chart gridline color and transparency.** chart_canvas gains grid (hex) and grid_opacity (0..1); minor gridlines follow the same hue at roughly half strength, and scatter-matrix panels (whose axes Plotly generates) are covered via template defaults - "make the gridlines pastel blue at 30%" is now a real instruction instead of something the AI improvises around. Applies to 2D axes and 3D scenes, kept separate from ink so a soft grid sits under crisp labels.
- **Styling specs cannot miss silently anymore.** An op naming a chart ref that does not exist used to be skipped without a word; now a red toast lists the unresolved refs and reminds that refs come from the chart manifest. Also: the produced_by instruction no longer contains an example model name for AIs to parrot.
- **The title slide gets the same selection-aware formatting.** Color, font, and size on the title slide description now apply to the selected text only (whole block when nothing is selected), with the same re-render-proof selection memory as chart notes.
- **Fix (final): note colors stay on the selection - the real root cause.** Clicking the color swatch blurs the note, and the note saves itself on blur - that save re-renders the text and silently destroyed the remembered selection before the picker even opened; every earlier fix was downstream of this. The selection is now also remembered as character positions, which survive any re-render: the picker rebuilds it against the fresh text and colors exactly your words. The note also no longer saves when focus merely moves to its own toolbar. Reproduced and verified against a real DOM, including selections crossing bold boundaries.
- **Fix: split box/violin plots keep their dots.** Adding a Split to a box plot silently switched the point cloud to outliers-only, so the dots seemed to vanish. Split sub-groups now follow the same Dots control and auto policy as unsplit charts (every point up to 10,000 rows).
- **Transparent charts lose the toolbar chrome too.** In transparent mode the chart-controls header bar stops painting its background so nothing white floats on your deck background.
- **Fix (round 2): note colors truly stay on the selection.** The first fix left a focus cascade (restoring the selection blurred the color picker, whose blur saved, whose save detached the selection - whole-note repaint one tick later). Selection styling no longer uses the browser focus machinery at all: the remembered selection is wrapped in a styled span directly, the picker restyles that same span on every tick, and one save happens when the picker closes. Verified against a real DOM: one span, only the selected words, survives saving.
- **Fix: note formatting respects your selection.** Coloring selected note text could suddenly repaint the whole note: the native color picker fires per hue-tick, each tick saved the note, and saving detached the remembered selection - later ticks then fell back to everything. Color now paints only while the picker is open and saves once when it closes; stale selections are detected instead of misfiring. Font and size gained selection-awareness for the first time (select text, pick a font or size - only that text changes; nothing selected changes the whole note), and font-family spans survive saving.
- **Per-chart canvas colors.** A single chart can now have its own background (including full transparency at opacity 0 - just that chart melts into the page), its own ink (axis lines, ticks, labels, captions - 3D scenes included), and its own legend text color, overriding the global Appearance settings. Built for exactly the transparent-chart-on-gradient look: make one chart transparent and hand it light ink so the text reads on your deck color. AI: the new chart_canvas op (bg / bg_opacity / ink / legend_color), disclosed in the chart manifest and round-tripped by Copy current styling.
- **Truly transparent charts.** Sliding the chart background opacity to 0 now means what it says: in Presentation view the plot AND its card stop painting entirely, so the deck page background (solid or gradient) is the chart real background. Any other opacity still blends over the card surface as before. Exports of transparent charts carry the transparency (PNGs get an alpha background).

## 2026-07-13

- **Save remembers your session name.** Saving a session under a chosen name now keeps that name: the next Save prefills it (instead of re-deriving the dataset filename), and the name travels inside the .clue - a reopened session still knows what it is called. Combined with the remembered save folder, repeat saves go to the same file with one confirmation.
- **Notes move as freely as images.** Every presentation note gains a drag grip (hover the note, top-left): grab it and the note converts to FREE placement - drop it anywhere on its chart row, with the same blue snap guides as charts and images, on both axes. A right-edge handle resizes it in place. The L/R/T/B buttons return it to a column; the AI can place freely too (chart_preso_note position "free" with x/y, in px or band percents).
- **Unpivot columns are editable live.** While the unpivoted view is on, the column chips stay visible - tap to add or remove measures and the view recomputes on the spot (filters carry over, each change gets a lens-journal line). Plus All/Reset links for quick selection.
- **Bars stopped being enormous.** A bar chart with only one or a few categories let each bar swallow the plot. Bars now auto-narrow as categories get fewer (one lone bar takes ~20% of the plot instead of 80%; grouped bars narrow less since they share the slot). Setting the Width % knob overrides the automatic choice, as before.
- **Unpivot - as a view, not an edit.** The Fields tab gains an Unpivot panel: pick two or more measure columns, name the pair (Series/Value by default), and flip "Use unpivoted view" - every chart now reads the long form, where the measures become a category you can group, race, facet, or filter by. The stored table is untouched: Source, the journal, signatures, and reconciliation all still describe your original wide data, and the switch is recorded in the lens journal like a filter change. Each view keeps its own charts and filters - flip back and your wide charts return exactly as you left them. The AI context discloses the active view in all three modes.
- **The most-asked styling knobs, unlocked.** Bar+line combos: the overlay line gets a color swatch in the toolbar (line, dots, and its axis text follow), plus width and dash via AI (chart_bars line_color / line_width / line_dash). Error bars: color, thickness, and cap width (chart_bars error_color / error_thickness / error_cap). Donuts: the hole is adjustable (chart_display donut_hole, 0 = full pie) and pie/donut slices can show values instead of percents (pie_text). Box and violin plots: a Dots control - Auto (every point up to 10,000 rows, then outliers), All, Outliers, or None. Everything round-trips through Copy current styling and is disclosed in the AI context.
- **Candle colors are yours.** The candlestick toolbar gains two color swatches - up (closed at or above the open) and down - defaulting to the same green/red as before. The AI can set them too: chart_display with ohlc_up_color / ohlc_down_color.
- **Big files stopped being laggy.** A 54,000-row dataset (ggplot2 diamonds) was re-processed almost every time you touched anything; now work only happens when something that actually affects a chart changes. Chart data is cached and rebuilt only on real changes (filters, edits, styling, selection); off-screen charts no longer pay rendering costs; scatters above 2,500 points draw on the graphics card instead of as 54,000 separate page elements; Smooth (LOESS) trendlines fit on a deterministic 1,500-point sample past that size and say so in their caption; box-plot dots fall back to outliers-only above 10,000 rows. And datasets too large for the browser to auto-save no longer pay for the failed attempt on every change - a notice tells you auto-recovery is off and to use Save.
- **The suggested scatter matrix now picks the RIGHT five columns.** With more than five numeric columns, the "All measures at a glance" suggestion used to take the first five in file order - which could keep weakly-related ratios and drop the interesting ones (diamonds: it kept depth and table, dropped the y/z dimensions). It now keeps the five that relate most strongly to the other numerics (highest mean |r|, from the correlation scan it already runs), shown in dataset order, and says so. A matrix added manually from the chart-type menu still shows all numeric columns.
- **Candlestick / OHLC charts.** Over Time gains the financial classic: pick Open, High, Low, and Close columns (auto-guessed from the near-universal column names) and get green-up/red-down candles - or flip a toolbar switch to classic OHLC bars. Dates with several rows aggregate honestly and deterministically: open = first, high = max, low = min, close = last; no values are invented. Works with the timeline zoom slider, log scale, and manual Y ranges, and the AI builds them too (`candlestick` with `open/high/low/close` encodings, `chart_display` → `"ohlc_style": "bars"`).
- **Images snap to the grid of everything else.** Free-dragged images get the same blue alignment guides as charts and notes: edges snap to chart cards, note columns, and other images while moving or resizing - and charts and notes snap back to images in return.
- **Deck images move freely.** Grab a placed image and drag it ANYWHERE on its chart's row - it converts to free placement wherever you drop it, with a corner handle for resizing (the left/right/top/bottom slots remain as starting points, with an edge handle for in-slot sizing). The AI can place freely too: deck_image position "free" with x/y.
- **Fix: "Image beside Chart" now appears in every chart's ⋮ menu.** A wiring slip left the new menu item on only one chart per group; all charts have it now.
- **Fix: "match" notes no longer inherit a position offset.** Copying a note's styling with `"match"` also copied the source note's band offset (often left over from a drag) - harmless while charts were centered, but once charts are pinned on a grid it parked the copied note on top of them. `match` now copies styling only (font, size, color, width, gap, position); offsets stay per-chart. Note `"left"` also accepts band percents now (`"8.3%"` = the start of column 2), matching chart geometry.

## 2026-07-12

- **Smooth trendlines (LOESS).** Scatter charts get a trend-style picker: the classic straight line with its formula, **Smooth** - a LOESS curve with a 95% ribbon that follows the data's curvature, exactly ggplot's `geom_smooth` - or **Smooth only**, hiding the points for the pure relationship view ("do heavier penguins have longer flippers?" answered in one clean curve). Per-series when grouped, deterministic, self-captioned ("LOESS, span 0.75, n=342") when the formula toggle is on. AI: `chart_display` → `"trend_style": "smooth"`.
- **Images in your deck.** Appearance gains **Images (deck assets)**: link a folder (or add files) and Clues embeds resized copies (max 1280px, ~WebP) *inside the session* - decks stay self-contained, images travel with every shared `.clue`. Descriptions come from filenames or an optional `captions.json`. Place one from any chart's ⋮ menu → **Image beside Chart** (left/right/above/below, width in px or band-percent, shown in Presentation view like notes), and a running size meter warns before the session gets heavy. The AI sees the library in its context (names + descriptions) and places images itself with the new `deck_image` op - "put the team logo right of the KPI chart" now just works. Images *inside* charts (pinned at data coordinates) are the next phase.
- **Deck layouts in percentages.** Chart widths and positions (`chart_box`) and presentation-note widths now accept percents of the presentation band - `"width": "58.3%", "left": "33.3%"` - resolved live on whatever screen the deck is shown on, and re-resolved when the window changes. A 12-column grid layout survives the jump from your monitor to a projector with its proportions intact, and the AI's placement-geometry instructions now need no pixel arithmetic at all. Plain px still work everywhere; dragging an edge converts that chart to fixed px.
- **Decompose a time series.** A new toggle on time-series charts splits each line into stacked **Observed / Trend / Seasonal / Residual** panels on one shared time axis - classical decomposition, computed deterministically from your data (seasonality from the date cadence, honest gaps where the centered window doesn't fit, residual panel with a zero line). It's the diagnostic view: see what drives the series before trusting any forecast. Needs at least 7 time points; below that the chart stays a normal line. AI: `chart_display` → `"decompose": true`.
- **The lens has a memory now.** Changes to the *view* - filter edits ("2 of 3 species kept, unchecked Adelie"), quant and date range limits, selection rules switching on or off, column-type changes - are recorded as timestamped lens-history entries, debounced so a slider drag is one entry, capped at 200, and saved with the session. The Audit-trail context lists the most recent alongside the current state, so "was this chart ever shown with different filters?" finally has an answer. Kept deliberately separate from the data journal - signatures and reconciliation are untouched.
- **The audit context discloses the lens.** For anyone auditing charts, filters and type changes matter as much as edits: a filtered-out row is as invisible as a deleted one, and a retyped column changes what every aggregation means. The Audit-trail context reports active filters and selection rules, column-type overrides, and ignored columns.

## 2026-07-11

- **Ask your AI "what happened to this data?"** *Copy AI context* gains a third mode: **Audit trail**.
